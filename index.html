Hugo

Growing up with deaf family members is definitely something I'm not familiar with, run me down some things in your daily life that you felt would be different if your parents could hear.

Josh

Wow, it's actually surreal thinking about how different things would be. From little things like not having to sign for my parents when they want to order food at a restaurant, not having lights flash when the phone or doorbell is ringing, my friends and my parents having more of a relationship rather than just "hi", "hi", "how are you", etc; my parents being friends with my friend's parents, not having to constantly get my moms attention with my hand when we are in the same room, definitely not being able to get away with blasting music in my room at 2am or singing loudly when I'm driving with my mom/dad, we definitely wouldnt have the vcr which is like facetime but for deaf people where they have a speaking sign language interpreter on the other end for when they have to call places like the doctor's office, insurance, school's, etc. Obviously I make it work and have been so accustomed to so many things that I'm probably forgetting. But there are things like emotional connections and being able to really talk about how I feel without having to write it down sometimes as there are lots of feelings that don't have the right sign word to capture that feeling. I remember signing for my parents at the doctors, being at my own parent-teacher conferences to sign for my teachers, and honestly just having to grow up a lot faster than other kids my age.

Give me some more detail about each place you've lived, what were the people like, how was the environment, any significant landmarks?

Josh

Philadelphia, Pa; Just very urban and cold. I have very limited memories from this period of my life but a significant story from this time was my pre-school burning down. Haha everyone ended up safe but I can vividly remember being woken up from nap time in a panic and having to be led out with all my classmates to a building across the street. I remember leaving school that day and seeing all the ash from the remains, especially a Thomas the train pillow that was barely recognizable. We ended up holding school at a nearby building in the area for the remainder of the year. Bonita Springs, FL; this was such a great time to go through the early grades of the k-12 system. The weather was always so humid and warm, with nice light chilly falls/ winters. I remember having all the hallways be basically outdoors with awnings above. The kids were very lively and spontaneous and just had so much energy (i mean which kid doesn't at kindergarten, first grade). I don't really remember any significant landmarks besides the mini deserts/ fields around my house and the one time I got bed bugs from playing in a field. Paramus, NJ: Where I live now is definitely what I would call my hometown. I've lived in three different houses in just this one town alone, the first two were rented and the last is where my family has finally purchased our home. I really came to true form living here through the many art programs held at my middle and high schools. The people here are definitely higher income compared to the people in Florida. A LOT of privilege, ignorance, money, but also pretty intelligent, liberal, and artistic kids sprinkled throughout. Classic suburbia with alot of urbanized elements (5 shopping malls, 10+ Starbucks, huge highway with shops all alongside) and I guess a significant landmark is seeing the NYC skyline in the distance lol.

You seem to have several scars, but the one that interests me is the one behind your left ear. How'd you get that one?

Josh

I got this scar when I was about 3 years old. I was living in Philadelphia at the time with my parents and my great aunt, Tita Pat. I was playing trampoline on a sofa that was right near a large window with an ac radiator right beneath it. As I fell my ear hit the corner of the radiator that had a sharp edge to it and it ended up leaving a 2-inch scar right behind my left ear. My mom was mortified.
